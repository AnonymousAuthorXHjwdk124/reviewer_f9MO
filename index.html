Question 3: Mitigation of Local Minima in Training

The two figures present plots corresponding to the "Girl with Pearl Earring" (top) and "Tokyo" 4K images (bottom), showcasing the performance of our PuTT method with upsampling versus a baseline QTT model without upsampling. For both images, we plot the average PSNR over 4,000 iterations, averaged across three seeds. The shaded regions around each curve represent the variability within ±1 standard deviation from the mean PSNR, serving as an indicator of training stability. In both plots it is evident that the upsampling model exhibits lower fluctuation in standard deviation and achieves higher PSNR levels by the end of the training period. This highlights the improved stability and effectiveness of our upsampling approach in 2D compression tasks.

Question 5: Computational Complexity and Efficiency of PuTT

This figure illustrates the computational requirements for sampling all values from a QTT using a single contraction, presented in a log-log scale. Sampling all points from a QTT with a rank of 50 for a 16k RGB image necessitates approximately 1011 FLOPs, while a similar QTT for a 2k RGB image requires around 109 FLOPs. The plot distinctly highlights the difference in FLOPs required for contractions at lower versus higher resolutions. Training at lower resolutions for half of the total iterations significantly reduces computational demands as it involves sampling from QTTs that require far fewer FLOPs. The choice of the iteration at which to upsample to the full resolution presents a trade-off between the benefits of lower resolution learning and computational savings.

Question 9: Comparison of Training on a Fraction of Input with Previous Work.

This figure presents a comparison of tensor train methods on the Barbara image (256x256x3), using only 1% of the available data. The first row displays the original Barbara image and the corresponding 1% sampled data. The second row compares the performance of our PuTT method with a batch size of 1024 against a QTT model without upsampling, using batch sizes of 1 and 1024, respectively. Our PuTT method achieves a PSNR of 16.72 with just 103 iterations. In contrast, the best PSNR attained by the non-upsampling QTT model is 15.52 after 106 iterations, and notably, when the batch size is increased to 1024 for the non-upsampling model, the PSNR drops to 6.52 after 106 iterations, underscoring PuTT's enhanced performance and efficiency.
